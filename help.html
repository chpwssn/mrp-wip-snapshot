Reference Guide WIP Shortage Snapshot

This document explains how to read the WIP Shortage Snapshot report, what each section and status means, and where the data comes from. Keep this alongside your printed report during floor walks.
Applies to report version 0.2.0 — check the version in your report header to confirm this guide matches.

1. Quick Reference & Terminology

TermMeaning
W JO (e.g., W9715)A Work Order — a customer production job. These are the primary focus of the report.
I JO (e.g., I4926)An Internal Job Order — builds assemblies or stock. Not directly for a customer.
BOMBill of Materials — the list of parts needed for a JO.
Blind SpotA BOM line with no plan: not kitted, no PO, no requisition. The core problem this report surfaces.
A-prefix part (e.g., A8800-004)An assembly part, typically built by an I JO.
Phantom / Shop SupplyConsumable parts (fittings, screws, etc.) consumed from bulk inventory, never formally issued to JOs. Identified by M2M fgroup = "SUPPLY" or fprodcl = "SS".
POPurchase Order in M2M.
Sys POSystem-wide PO — an open PO for a part anywhere in M2M, regardless of which JO it's routed to. May be routed to inventory, another JO, or an SO.
— (dash)No data / zero / not applicable. Used in POs, Sys POs, and On Hand columns when there's nothing to show.
Negative "Have"The M2M qty-issued field (fqty_iss) can go negative due to returns or adjustments. This is a data quality issue in M2M, displayed as-is.
Snapshot #Sequential run counter. Each time the collector runs, it creates a new snapshot. Compare snapshots over time to see if shortages are improving or worsening.

On screen, section headers are color-coded for quick scanning:

  • Amber — System-Wide Shortfall
  • Red — Blind Spots & Blocked Assemblies
  • Orange — Requisitioned
  • Gray — Stock Available

2. Data Sources

The report cross-references six independent systems. No single source is the "truth" — each has its own blind spots. The report surfaces where they agree and where they disagree or have gaps.

Made2Manage (M2M)

What: ERP system of record. Job Orders (JOMAST), BOMs (JODBOM), Purchase Orders (POMAST/POITEM), Inventory (INMASTX/INONHD), Routing (JODRTG).

Via: m2m-graphql-us.wasson.tech GraphQL API

Provides: BOM demand, qty issued, direct-to-JO POs, part metadata (group, product class), on-hand inventory, routing/work centers.

System POs (Redbook-style)

What: All open Purchase Orders in M2M for a given part number, regardless of which JO they're routed to.

Via: getPOItemsWhere GraphQL query per blind-spot part

Provides: System-wide PO coverage, overdue PO detection. Shown in the "Sys POs" column.

The Tool (Order Tracking)

What: Production tracking spreadsheet (Order Tracking MASTER NEW.xlsx). Maintained by production coordination.

Via: Direct file read from /Volumes/Documentation/

Provides: Customer name, production phase, kitting notes, build schedule, quoted delivery (col S), order entry date (col Q), ship target.

Notion Travelers

What: Notion database with one page per JO tracking lifecycle from Pre-Sales through Shipping.

Via: Notion REST API

Provides: Lifecycle status (Kitting, Production, Application, QC, etc.), Blocker text, Priority, Promised Ship Date. Also provides customer/project name as fallback.

Jira Purchasing (PUR)

What: Purchasing requisition tracking. Requisitions are submitted via email forwarding, with part details in PDF/XLSX attachments.

Via: Jira REST API + attachment download & parsing

Provides: Open requisitions per JO (upgrades blind spots to "Requisitioned"), parsed line items from PDF req forms and XLSX kitting sheets.

Jira Attachment Parser

What: Extracts structured data from Jira attachment files.

Formats parsed: Employee Req Form (fillable PDF), MRP Req Form (fillable PDF), Column Call-Out (text PDF), GC Ordering Sheet (XLSX), Post-WO Kitting (XLSX), Requisition Form (XLSX).

Provides: Part-level req annotations on blind spots ("Req'd but not on PO").

5. Report Sections

JOs appear in exactly one section, prioritized from most urgent to least:

SectionWhat it meansAction needed
System-Wide Shortfall Parts where total open demand across ALL JOs exceeds all known supply (direct POs + system POs + on-hand inventory). These parts need purchasing attention regardless of individual JO status. One PO could clear multiple JOs.
Blind Spots & Blocked Assemblies JOs with BOM lines that have no plan: no kitting, no PO, no requisition. Also includes JOs blocked by I JO assembly shortages. Highest priority. Each line needs a decision: order it, kit from stock, or escalate.
Requisitioned JOs where all blind spots matched to an open Jira PUR requisition (not yet Confirmed). Follow up with purchasing — req exists but PO may not have been created yet.
Stock Available (needs kitting) JOs where all shortages could be covered by existing on-hand inventory. These are kitting issues, not supply issues. Parts exist in inventory but haven't been transferred to the JO.
On Order JOs where all shortages are covered by existing POs (direct-to-JO). Monitor PO delivery dates. No action unless POs are overdue.
No Action Needed All BOM lines are complete, overissued, phantom, or assembly-done. None — materials are covered.
Internal Job Orders (Assemblies) I JOs that are referenced by W JOs for assembly parts (A-prefix). Shows their own BOM shortage status. If blocked (🔴), the parent W JO can't complete until the I JO's shortages are resolved.
Stock Build I JOs I JOs building to inventory — not tied to a specific W JO but have unresolved shortages. Lower priority but affects future availability. These build inventory buffers.

6. BOM Line Statuses

COMPLETE

Total supplied (qty issued + PO received) meets or exceeds the extended quantity needed. No action required.

ON_ORDER

A PO exists routed directly to this JO and the ordered quantity (including what's still in transit) covers the gap. Waiting on delivery.

PARTIAL

A PO exists but doesn't fully cover the gap. Some quantity is on order, but more is needed.

BLIND_SPOT

Nothing accounts for this part: not kitted, no PO routed to this JO, no requisition found. This is the core problem the report is designed to surface.

REQUISITIONED

Was a blind spot, but an open Jira PUR requisition was found that references this JO. The req exists but purchasing may not have created a PO yet.

STOCK_AVAILABLE

A blind spot where on-hand inventory (from INMASTX.INONHD) could cover the gap. The parts exist in the warehouse but haven't been issued/transferred to this JO. This is a kitting problem, not a supply problem.

PHANTOM

Shop supply / consumable part (INMASTX fgroup = "SUPPLY" or fprodcl = "SS"). These are consumed from bulk inventory and reconciled quarterly — they are never formally issued to individual JOs. Not a real shortage.

MAKE_BLOCKED

This is an assembly part (A-prefix) with a corresponding I JO that has its own blind spots. The parent W JO is blocked until the I JO's shortages are resolved. A cascading shortage.

MAKE_NO_JO

An assembly part (A-prefix) with no corresponding released I JO found in the system. Either the I JO hasn't been created, or it's in a different status.

MAKE_COMPLETE

Assembly part whose I JO has all BOM lines complete.

MAKE_IN_PROGRESS

Assembly part whose I JO is in progress — has on-order or stock-available items but no blind spots.

OVERISSUED

More has been supplied (issued + received) than the extended quantity requires. May indicate a data quality issue or intentional overage.

8. Icons & Annotations

⚠️
Overdue PO — A PO where the required/promise date has passed but quantity remains open. Format: PO 051356 → INV (WIL001) 30 open, 8d overdue
  • 051356 — M2M PO number
  • → INV — Destination: INV = inventory, W#### = a specific JO, SO = a sales order
  • (WIL001) — M2M vendor code (e.g., WIL001 = Wilmad/Wilson)
  • 30 open — Qty ordered minus qty received
  • 8d overdue — Days past the due/promise date
📋
Req'd but not on PO — A parsed Jira req attachment contains this part number, but no system PO exists. Format: PUR-5789 (PO Sent) 5ea→INV
  • PUR-5789 — Jira issue key
  • (PO Sent) — Current Jira workflow status (see Section 12)
  • 5ea — Quantity from the parsed attachment
  • →INV — Destination from the req form (INV, a JO number, or free-text like WO yet to exist)
Only shown when there are no system POs for the part. "Confirmed" reqs are excluded (historical).
🔴
BLOCKED (on assembly lines) — This assembly part's I JO has its own blind spots. Shows the I JO number, completion percentage, and blind spot count. See the "Internal Job Orders" section for detail.
🔴
No I JO found (on assembly lines) — This A-prefix part has no released internal Job Order building it.

I JO Header Icons

In the "Internal Job Orders" and "Stock Build I JOs" sections, each I JO header has a traffic-light emoji:

🔴
Red circle — This I JO has blind spots. It's blocked and can't complete until shortages are resolved.
🟡
Yellow circle — This I JO has no blind spots but has items in progress (on order, stock available, etc.). It's moving forward.

9. Assemblies & Internal JOs

Parts starting with "A" are assemblies. They may be built by internal Job Orders (I-prefix, e.g., I4926). The report evaluates each I JO's BOM the same way it evaluates W JOs:

  • If the I JO's BOM is fully complete → the assembly line on the W JO is MAKE_COMPLETE
  • If the I JO has items on order but no blind spots → MAKE_IN_PROGRESS
  • If the I JO has its own blind spots → MAKE_BLOCKED (cascading shortage)
  • If no released I JO exists for the part → MAKE_NO_JO

The "Internal Job Orders (Assemblies)" section at the end of the report shows detail for each I JO referenced by a W JO. The "Stock Build I JOs" section shows I JOs building to inventory that aren't tied to a specific W JO.

10. Kitting Status

Internal Job Orders may have a KITTING work center in their M2M routing (JODRTG). An I JO cannot move to the production floor until kitting is complete. The report shows:

⬜
Kitting: Not Started — The kitting operation has zero quantity completed. Shows how many are to-go.
🟨
Kitting: In Progress — Some quantity has been kitted, but more remains. Shows completed and to-go counts.
✅
Kitting: Complete — All required quantity has been kitted. The I JO can proceed to production.

W Job Orders typically do not have a KITTING routing operation — their kitting is managed by MRP outside of the routing workflow.

11. System-Wide Shortfall

The "System-Wide Shortfall" table at the top of the report takes a different view from the per-JO analysis. It aggregates all open demand for each part across every JO and compares it against all known supply:

Shortfall = Open Need − Direct POs − System POs − On Hand

ColumnMeaning
Open NeedSum of (extended qty − qty issued − PO received) across all JOs where this part is short. Excludes phantom, make, and complete lines.
Direct POSum of qty still on order on POs routed directly to JOs.
Sys POOpen qty on system-wide POs (may overlap with Direct PO for parts ordered to inventory).
On HandCurrent on-hand inventory.
ShortfallThe gap after accounting for all supply. If positive, there is no way to cover all demand from existing stock and orders.

Note: Direct PO and Sys PO may overlap for parts ordered to inventory — the same PO could appear in both columns. The shortfall number may therefore be conservative (actual shortfall could be slightly higher). Use it as a directional indicator, not a precise count.

A single PO for a high-shortfall part can clear blind spots on many JOs simultaneously.

12. Jira Requisition Statuses

Jira PUR requisitions move through a workflow. The report shows the current status in annotations and the Requisitioned section:

StatusCategoryWhat it means
Requisition ReceivedTo DoReq just arrived. Purchasing hasn't started working on it.
Under reviewTo DoRequisition is awaiting approval for funds.
Awaiting MRP approvalIn ProgressReq needs MRP manager sign-off before purchasing can proceed.
To DoTo DoApproved for PO creation, but purchasing hasn't created it yet.
PO SentIn ProgressPO has been sent to the vendor. Note: the PO may not yet appear in M2M, or it may be routed to inventory (invisible at JO level). If you see "Req'd but not on PO" with status "PO Sent", check whether the PO was entered into M2M.
ConfirmedDoneReq is complete — PO was created, sent, and confirmed. Excluded from the report (treated as historical).
RejectedDoneReq was rejected. Excluded from the report.

Why "PO Sent" shows as "Req'd but not on PO": The annotation checks M2M for open POs matching the part number. If the PO was routed to inventory (blank fjokey) and filtered by the system PO query, it should show in Sys POs instead. If you see both "PO Sent" and no Sys PO, the PO may not have been entered into M2M yet, or it was entered under a different part number/revision.

13. Known Limitations

Inventory-routed POs are invisible at the JO level

When parts are ordered into general inventory (blank fjokey), the link between the PO and the JO it was meant for is lost. The "Sys POs" column partially compensates by showing system-wide open POs, but we can't tell which JO a system PO was intended for. The Jira req parser helps reconstruct intent from attachment data.

On-hand inventory may be committed elsewhere

STOCK_AVAILABLE means on-hand qty exists, but that stock may already be earmarked for other JOs. The report cannot see M2M allocations.

Jira requisition matching is text-based

JO numbers are extracted from Jira issue summaries, descriptions, and parsed attachments via regex. Some requisitions may not mention the JO number, and false matches are possible. "Confirmed" status requisitions are excluded as historical.

Phantom detection relies on part metadata

Parts are classified as phantom/shop supply based on fgroup = "SUPPLY" or fprodcl = "SS" in INMASTX. If a part isn't tagged correctly in M2M, it won't be filtered.

The Tool's quoted delivery is free-text

Column S in the Order Tracking spreadsheet sometimes contains dates, sometimes narrative text (e.g., "Bounced to", "End of Summer?"). The report displays whatever is in the field.

Data sources may conflict

Notion might say "Production" while The Tool says "Lab/QC". The report shows both without resolving the conflict — use your judgment to determine which is current.
